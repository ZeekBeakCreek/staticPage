About the blob

The Blobfish (rather than ‘blob fish’) is a deep-sea fish which inhabits waters just above the sea bed at depths of 600 to 1,200 meters (2,000 to 3,900 feet), off the coasts of mainland Australia, New Zealand and Tasmania.

Not always ugly

The blobfish actually looks like a completely normal fish in its usual environment.

Blobfish was first discovered in 2003, and immediately given it’s name.

Yes, it’s real name is ‘Blobfish’, it’s not a nickname. The very first blobfish ever found was nicknamed ‘Mr. Blobby’ by scientists and crew after being trawled during the NORFANZ expedition, on the Norfolk Ridge, north-west of New Zealand.
